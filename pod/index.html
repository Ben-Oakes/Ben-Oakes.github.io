Pod Save America

A review by Benjamin H Oakes

Proceed to review

Pod Saves America dives deep into the warzone that is American Politics

Pod Saves America is an expertly made bi-weekly podcast that covers the seething hellscape that is the American political climate in 2018. Hosted by former aides for former president Obama, it offers insightful, if not biased, political wisdom on what is currently happening in the world of US politics. While it is certainly very informative and very entertaining, the bias that the hosts have due to their political careers has the potential to turn away the very audience that they want to change.

Formatting and Production Quality

I knew from the moment I heard the first ad read that the production quality in this podcast was going to be high. Ever podcast I’ve ever listened to treats advertisement reads as something that they just have to do, but the PSA cast actually has fun with it and makes it actually entertaining. My favorite one so far was at the very end of their most recent episode, Jon Lovett (NOTE TO SELF MAKE SURE IT WAS ACTUALLY LOVETT SAYING THIS AND NOT THE OTHER JOHN) was doing a normal ad for a life insurance company. while, in the background, Tommy Vietor was listing extinction level events that have happened throughout history and other catastrophic reasons you may need life insurance; it was extremely entertaining.

Ad reads aside, everything else about Pod Saves America is produced amazingly well. From the extremely fitting music that combines patriotic beats with chip tune music, to the incredibly organized transitions, everything seems like it was done with great care. Additionally, the podcast is recorded on a set and broadcasted on HBO, and has very well done visual effects. The delivery of lines and questions from the 4 hosts is always on point and the guest stars in each episode are edited in very naturally.

The actual format of the show is also great. Normally with a podcast that is up to an hour and thirty minutes long, it takes a lot of effort to keep the viewers attention. Pod Saves America combats this by making the show incredibly organized and cut up into distinct segments. For example, their most recent episode had 3 main segments: A short intro that lasts for about twenty minutes where they go over what they are going to be talking about and the Kavanaugh related news events that have occured since the last episode, a segment where two of the hosts had a sort of rant session with each other where they discussed the political fallout of Kavanaugh’s nomination with each other and discussed both why he won and what this means for America going forward, followed by a series of short clips from interviews they recently had with three to four celebrities knowledgeable on the issues. Each section is broken up with a short intermission, this style of formatting allows the reader to easily breaks and pick up later.

The meat (what you actually came here for)

Of course, tons of good podcasts have high production quality, what sets Pod Saves America apart from the competition is that the actual content of the podcast is able to stand out and is still great when you take the production away. While the great guest stars definitely help, the reason viewers keep coming back is because of the core four. Jon F, Jon L, Tommy, and Dan are all wonderful hosts who are both extremely knowledgeable about politics and extremely entertaining. Serving as aides to former President Barack Obama, not only are they extremely knowledgeable, but they have experience in the political field.

Even if you are not that interested in the issues being discussed, it is still entertaining to listen to the hosts as they are all great at keeping the listener engaged. They have a great sense of humor, though it is a depressing sense. None of the hosts hog the conversation and they don’t let the content get stale. Additionally, the group often has guests on the show that they interview, and they range from comedians to political journalists. At one point Jimmy Kimmel was interviewed, just in case you were curious how famous some of these guests are.

The red Elephant in the room

Of course, everything has a catch. Throughout this review, there has been an elephant in the room that I have yet to address. This elephant is big, red, and a metaphor for the Republican Party. If you have far right leaning views, you may not enjoy this podcast at first. The hosts are all very clear about their biases and do not try to hide it. While I would not classify them as having an undying loyality to any party, (they actually critisize the democratic party in their recent episode on why Kavanaugh won) they are definitely not loyal to the GOP. To be fair however, the hosts have stated that they aren't bothered by Republicans who simply have opposing political philosophies, and that the people they take issue with are the politicians themselves currently running the GOP. They are openly biased against Schumer, Kavanaugh, and Donald Trump. However, even if you do have conservative views, I would still recommend this podcast. If you can get past the bias there is some legitimate intelligent discussion happening.

Conclusion:

Overall, Pod Save America is a brilliantly produced podcast that is hosted by brilliant people. It strikes a perfect balance of being both informative and entertaining at the same time without having to water down the information. The hosts have great personalities and synergy, not to mention are experts in their field. The only real flaw is the fact that it is biased, but everything these days has bias. Though they are biased, the way they present the information is not. If you can get past the bias, it is a great companion to the Trump preisdency.

+ Great Production quality, formatting fits the content well and does a good job at keeping the listener's attention.

+ Hosts are experts in their field while also extremely entertaining.

- Hosts are heavily biased, but they are open about it.
